Hickory Bot Battles had a 3d printed only category in their competition. Hesitant to sacrifice robots to destructive opponents and wanting to start out I decided to build a small "brit" flipper for this category. 


Using a Prusa I iterated on the body and flipper mechanism. The flipper was driven by a servo through a four bar. This created an issue related to the slow actuation of the servo. Secondly the flipper spatual ended up not being able to remain consistently flush on the ground. 


To address this, just before the competition I glued an old version of the flipper spatula on the front of the chassis to keep it on the ground. This moved the flipper arm to being only able to lift after the wedge had lifted the opponent up.
