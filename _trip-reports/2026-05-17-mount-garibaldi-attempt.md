Plans solidified last minute that Alain would be able to climb Garibaldi so I drove up and met him in Squamish Saturday afternoon. We took his (AWD but low clearance) car up as far as we could on the Cheekeye FSR in the rain and camped at about 1100m where another team was stopped who said they were also attempting Garibaldi the next day.

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-1.JPEG)

When we left at about 3:30am, the sky had cleared and the other team was about twenty minutes ahead of us. We continued hiking up the rocky road until a while after we reached the well refrozen snow and continued skinning up to and down past the Brohm Ridge Chalet. The views of Garibaldi and the Tantalus range kept getting better as the sun rose and we made good time up to and along the ridge. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-2.JPEG)

We continued along the ridge with a combination of side hilling and going over snow banks at the top. We passed a group camping and shortly after a group of 3 climbers from UW who were struggling on the steep side hill (this was the first time using both the top and bottom most grips on my poles for me). We kept skinning until we got to where a cliff blocked the ridge where we descended through some old wet loose debris before regaining the ridge at the edge of the glacier. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-3.JPEG)

Here the other group of two that camped nearby was about to rope up and we realized that they were also at UW, were good friends with Alain's sister, and we all had a mutual friend in common. With this found out, we decided that a team of 4 was better than two teams of 2 and roped up together. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-4.JPEG)
![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-6.JPG)

We made good time on the glacier, noting the new snow, and decided to dig a pit before we got to the steep section which seemed relatively stable. We could see from below that the bergshcrund was quite open but there were a couple potential places to cross it. We aimed for the snow bridge to the right that looked to be on less steep ground and lead towards the summit slope.

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-5.JPEG)

We got up below the bergschrund and transitioned to crampons as a cloud rolled in. Unfortunately as a group we had a total of one picket, so Makena placed it above the bergschrund as she lead the group past it. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-8.JPEG)

We all successfully crossed it but the snow above was icier than below, leading to our realization that it would be downclimbing rather than skiing off the summit and that it was already getting close to the turnaround time. Alain and I also wish we had brought two tools rather than a single one each. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-7.JPEG)

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-9.JPEG)


We decided to turn around at this point and made it back to our skis as a  group reached our position and continued to the top. After transitioning and waiting a few minutes for the cloud to leave, we skied down the glacier and had some of the best runs of the season. We continued back, party skiing to the flat first section of the glacier. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-10.JPEG)

The path back along the ridge involved many transitions and sections of skinning until we finally reached back to the Chalets. We passed a few groups on snowshoes enjoying the scenery and weather. While we were out, someone had taken the snow cat that was parked near our cars and groomed the road down from the Chalet giving us fresh corduroy to rip down before the final hill and transitions. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-11.JPEG)

We made it back to the car around 4:30 for a 13 hour trip and the ability to get back to Seattle at a reasonable time. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/garibaldi-12.jpg)
