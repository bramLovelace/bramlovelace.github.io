I was able to leave early Friday and made the four hour drive from Seattle to the crater creek campsite in time to hike in that night. I hadn't been to this part of the state before so it was cool to see the Apple orchards and slightly more rugged than rolling hills covered in golden grasses with scattered trees along the Columbia. The trailhead was popular and had some people camping and dirt bikes around, but I got ready and started hiking. There were about a dozen of them that passed me at one point or another heading back to the cars but all were respectable and they were loud enough I could get out of their way but not so loud that it didn't fade as they left. 
![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-19-sawtooths/sawtooth-1.JPG)

I passed another group that was planning on climbing some of the peaks and made my way up the snow from Cooney lake to Switchback mountain which was the first tag of the trip. I also took my camera out and got some nice shots with the light almost in golden hour.

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-19-sawtooths/sawtooth-2.JPG)
![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-19-sawtooths/sawtooth-5.JPG)

Continuing on the ridge I made my way to Martin peak and got some even better pictures. With the sun rapidly setting I made my way down to the valley on the west side of the ridge and joined a trail towards Boiling lake where I set up camp. 

That evening I played around a little with astrophotography and caltopo. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-19-sawtooths/sawtooth-6.JPG)
![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-19-sawtooths/sawtooth-7.JPG)


I had originally planned to continue onto Bigelow, Raven Ridge, and Hoodoo before looping back to the car and then having sunday to either drive over to the star lake group and do another 22mi hike to tag those three or start working back and tag something like Cashmere in the Enchantments. Neither of these were ideal though as they would break up the days in an awkward manner so when I realized it would only be 8mi to get to Star peak from camp and I could tag it and courtney for a similar amount of hiking without an hour of driving in between I decided to attempt those saturday with the addon of Oval peak if I felt like it. 

Saturday I did not get an early start and ended up leaving camp at ~9:15am. Unfortunately the trail to Star lake involved about 2kft of descent extra and went down to the forest as low as 5500, but that meant I could run some of the trail, give or take all of the blow downs. I made good time and got to Star Peak at about 12:30, time for a short snack and photo break. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-19-sawtooths/sawtooth-8.JPG)

I continued down to the lake where I talked with a family on a day hike with their pair of dogs and back up to Courtney peak, on the way to which I met two other Bulger-bagging parties, neither of which seemed inclined to hike Star that day. Being only 2:30, I decided I had the time to go the extra 6mi and ~3000' of gain round trip for Oval peak, which turned out to be a mistake because the climbers trail switched between faint with blowdowns and just bushwhacking, adding 5 hours onto the total trip.

Despite this I made the summit before starting the long trek back to camp at boiling lake. I had not been able to make myself eat and drink as much as I should have because I did not bring enough simple sugars and so was either bonking or just struggling from the long day for the last 4 or 5 miles and 2kft up to camp, having to make frequent stops to force down a few bites and sips. I eventually got to camp around 12:30 and immediately went to sleep. 


The next morning I knew I wasn't going to get the three remaining peaks but decided that I had time and energy to climb ~1700' rather than ~700' to get to Bigelow peak rather than just the pass towards home. This took a few hours but I was able to make it 6 peaks for the weekend and leave only Raven and Hoodoo, which can be easily done as a pair from a different trailhead. After tagging the peak (which was not nearly as much of a scramble as I thought looking at the towers on the ridge) I traversed below the ridge down to the pass and continued out on the dirtbike trail to the car. I passed a few other hiking groups near the exit but no bikes.

When I got to my car I learned that I had picked up a screw and my rear driver tire was flat. I was about to put on the donut and risk it down the gravel road when one of the other hikers got back and told me they had a battery operated pump and so were able to inflate my tire back up. I checked many times coming down but it was holding pressure (despite the hissing sound) and I was able to make it the hour back to Chelan and to the walmart tire center right before it closed. Unfortunately they were unable to patch it but they helped me add tubeless slime to it and refill it so I could make it back over the pass. I grabbed food from the aptly named Quick Wok (and watched New Zealand, I think, score their one goal of their game) in Chelan before taking Alt 97 down (it was cool to see this columbia gorge from both sides) to Wenatchee(?) and getting cheap gas, at which point my tire was still holding most of the pressure it had been pumped with. I then came back over Stevens pass, checking air once again at the resort (down from 30 to 28psi but still good) and made it home after dark. The next day it still had enough air in it to drive to Les Schwab and get fully patched. 

The total trip ended up being over 50mi of hiking and 16kft of gain with 30mi and 10,300' of that being on Saturday.
