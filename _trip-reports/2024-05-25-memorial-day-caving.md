Thanks to some last minute organization, Drew and I (Bram) ended up going to the CRF Klamath project weekend at a cave in California. 

I drove down to Portland Thursday night and worked from Drew’s apartment Friday morning before we left around 1:30. After leaving the Willamette Valley, we didn’t hit traffic and were able to make it to the trailhead by 7:45pm. We hiked to the camp in an hour and a half, just barely needing headlamps at the end. We were met by a group of cavers around a fire and a few more trickled in later. 

On Saturday morning we left for the cave at ~9:45. We hiked the 2 miles to the cave and got into the cave at noon after everyone donned their gear and got ready. 

Joel Despain, the trip leader, had divided the group into 4 survey teams. The people who had been to the cave before led us through the slightly mazy entrance and into the lower levels. In a squeeze known as the Rasp, we saw a small giant salamander. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/scorpion-1.jpg)


Joel sent a team to work on the dig to connect to the Mansfield Entrance or Felipe’s pit. The others went to the big room. Mark’s (my) team started surveying and connected into the big room. We also identified a lead into a room below that I went down on a handline and explored slightly before ascending with difficulty. At this point I decided to go back to get our vertical gear which we had left at the bottom of the drop earlier in the cave to rig it while Drew and Mark continued surveying into the big room. I returned and we continued the survey of the big room. Drew went around the back to climb up to the second level and dropped a handline down to us so we could get up to it as well. We explored some leads in the top and surveyed up into a small crawl that I was the only one that would fit so I sketched that portion. 

We then went back to the big room where Joel had gotten to as well and drew went to check a lower lead and was gone for ~15 minutes before returning. Meanwhile I rigged the rope for the pit lead I had down climbed earlier and rappelled into it to see if it was worth doing now as mop up or saving. We decided to save it for the next day. 

We left the cave at about 6:30 and ran into Anastasiya on the hike back to camp, as well as a good number of fresh and tasty puffball mushrooms (thanks, Sierra!). After dinner and a nice campfire we headed to an early night dreaming karsty dreams. 

Sunday morning we got off slightly earlier and entered the cave by 11:20. Getting back to the big room was relatively straightforward and while we waited for the others to join us, Anastasiya and I crawled through a highly decorated passage that was an upper extension of canyons below. We could see people climbing the handline as well as earlier on in the cave, looking down from the cathedral room, a highly decorated room with a small pool and many rimstone dams. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/scorpion-3.jpg)

Once our teams arrived we went to the big room and rigged the pit lead from the previous day and surveyed down into it. We had an extra book so I sketched profile while Mark sketched plan. 

We found multiple places to the left where there is a room below but it is too small for me to fit. I was able to fit my head in one section but not my body. We then surveyed into a small formation room that pinches off but has a keyhole through which you can see a room below but is not human passable without some modification. Mark then headed up the rope while drew checked out one last crawling lead under the drop. This turned out to connect into the passage right below the hand line. We couldn’t find any stations so we left a piece of flagging and a cairn for RT45 on the wall to tie into later. 

We got to the big room with another team and started surveying the left side lead drew had identified earlier. Joel’s team (minus Michael who had headed out, plus Sierra's team) leapfrogged ahead of us and surveyed the end of it and a loop. 

With too many cavers surveying a small delicate passage all but Jason, Joel, and Sierra left to return to the big room. Anna’s team had also returned to the big room from exploring windy pit so we played around with taking some pictures before trickling towards the entrance. 

We left the cave at about 6:20 and hiked back to camp for more dinner and a bigger fire. 

On Monday we got a lazy start and were thankful for the sun's ability to dry off our gear (who knew cave suits grew on trees?). The group going north stopped in Grant's Pass for a meal before heading the rest of the way home. 

Mark, Drew, and I surveyed 593 ft and the total survey for the trip was 1328 ft, bringing the length of the cave with the current survey to just under 3700 ft!
