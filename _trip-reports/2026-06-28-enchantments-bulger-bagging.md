After the success of the peakbagging weekend in the Sawtooths the week prior and with the weather not looking good for the Portlanders to drive up to Washington Pass, I decided to take one day and attempt to link the nontechnical Enchantments Bulgers, following some tracks that other peakbaggers had done in the previous weeks. I camped at the ever popular Colchuck lake trailhead and talked with the volunteers in the morning about my plans.  

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-1.JPEG)

I made my way up the first part of the Stuart / Colchuck trail until the horse ford sign where I took off on a climbers trail to the left through the woods and worked through a series mellow bad benches with surprisingly easy bushwhacking where there wasnt a trail until I got to the boulder field. Above the boulder field was the first real cliff band which was relatively easily scramblable, leading towards more open forest. I continued up and bushwhacked through some more junper or other evergreen shrubs and passed one party who was also climbing Cannon. I did the last few sections of scrambling and came upon the log book under a cairn below the final slab. After standing on the slab, I signed the log book. It had taken about 4 hours to get the 5kft up to the peak. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-2.JPEG)
![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-3.JPEG)

I continued down through the lost world plateau and stayed right of Prusik pass, electing to climb a snow filled couloir onto the ridge of Enchantment peak. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-4.JPEG)

The snow was soft enough I used my ice axe but not crampons. I passed another mountaineer who had seen me coming down from Cannon before making it to Enchantment and tagging it. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-5.JPEG)

I had brought more sugar today and was periodically having nerds clusters and water and feeling much better than the previous Saturday. After this relatively easy tag, I returned down to a few lakes and took the main trail across the outflow of Leprechaun lake and headed up the snowfield to Mclellan peak. I think I could have gone at the inflow to the lake and it may have been slightly more efficient but this was not bad. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-6.JPEG)
![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-7.JPEG)

I followed some tracks up the snow and scrambled around behind the summit to the final block which was the most real scramble of all the peaks I climbed that day. I found by wrapping around the summit block there was a weakness I could scramble up without bad exposure. On the summit the wind was starting to pick up so I returned to a sheltered spot and refueled before descending to the snow. Since I knew the next section was mostly on snow I put on my crampons and traversed down to Enchantment pass, about 80% of which was on snow. Unfortunately right before the pass I stuck my leg through a snow bridge and got my left foot soaked in a melt stream. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-8.JPEG)

Keeping moving, my foot was cold but not dangerously so, so I elected to continue up around the lake and on towards Little Annapurna. I climbed a steep snow gulley that had a single step of near vertical snow at the top, getting to a mellow incline with a few tracks. The wind was considerable at this point and dark clouds started to be pushed into Dragontail so I made my way as quickly as I could up the long slog to little Annapurna. The top of which (Along with the lost world plateau earlier) reminded me of the rock structures in Dartmoor. 

From there I descended to the pass between Little Annapurna and West Annapurna where I sheltered under a large rock to eat and make my next plan. I had thought of traversing to Dragontail before descending Asgard if I had time, but with the wind and clouds and my wet foot I decided it would be prudent to return to the trail and down Asgard. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-9.JPEG)

On the trail, I ran into a few parties that had gotten camping permits and they told me there was no snow below where I was on Asgard, which I took as my cue to find another shelter rock and change into my trail runners. Thankfully Marc had convinced me to bring them after Dragontail so I was able to switch into dry socks and shoes and dry my feet off (which ended up not being that cold / wet, thankfully) and start anew down Asgard Pass. As much as it gets complained about, it is not a terrible path other than that it is a slog with some loose kitty litter. I passed a team that had climbed the serpentine Arete and then made it to the lake where I passed a number of folks from the Olympia mountaineers who had gone up the Colchuck glacier to climb Colchuck and Dragontail. I made good time hiking quickly and running the trail to he car but still made it back only around dark. I was thankful that I hadn't climbed Dragontail when I made it home after midnight. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/2026-06-28-enchantments-bulgers/enchantments-10.JPEG)

The day ended up at 19mi and 10kft in just under 14hrs car to car. This was the second 10kft day in as many weeks and also my second 10kft vert day ever for me. I was really happy with my performance through mixed terrain and linking peaks, I hope I can continue to do this type of day in the future.
