The eight of us piled into Alex Krolick's and my (Bram Lovelace) cars to head to the busy Trail of Two Forests parking lot as we were the only ones with Sno-Park passes. Leaving Cygnus at the car due to the ladder in the cave, we hiked past the latrines and through the snowy forest to the conspicuous hole in the ground that marks Lake Cave. 

The group had a relatively relaxed time entering the cave and stopped for pictures and to notice all the cave had to offer. The water in the cave was relatively high, with a small stream most of the way and 3 distinct places with significant pools. Sarah pointed out how arrows graffitied on the walls with spray paint killed the cave slime with a zone of exclusion around it (see photo below).

Thanks to the nature of the cave, it had a little bit of everything for people to explore. Different portions of the group did their own thing, some continuing to the eponymous lake at the bottom of the cave, others choosing to explore intriguing erosional side passages with tree casts. 

On the way out we made sure to gather everyone back together and returned everyone to their cars at the dam. Those of us not headed south immediately debriefed over Thai food in Woodland before returning home. 

----

After our adventure in Cougar on Friday, the group realized we had a good opportunity to attend the Oregon Grotto meeting as Vancouver was only an hour away. After the meeting we solicited for suggestions on what to do Saturday and Randolph Miller suggested that we join him on his video trip to see the well-decorated Kim's Cave. Agreeing to a time to meet and what we thought was the same parking location, we went our separate ways that night. 

At 10:00 the next morning (Saturday, March 16th, 2024), Sarah Burgess, Bram Lovelace, Saynier Tormire, Kelden Andrews, Sri Rangarajan, and Cygnus met with Alex Krolick, Alex Kennedy, and Zack Robinson at the lay-by under the power lines that led to the cave. We got ready at a leisurely pace and waited for Randolph until about 10:30 before setting off into the woods towards the gps point of the cave, figuring that he would be able to find his way to the cave as he had originally planned. 

After hiking up the trail onto the basalt flow, we spotted Randolph in the distance behind us and he led us to the cave since he knew the entrances. Unbeknownst to us, Randolph had waited at the Cougar rest stop waiting for us to join him and when we didn't show up there decided to bring his large camera and gimbal to have a leisurely solo recording day to gather high quality footage for his youtube channel. 

We entered at the upper entrance and the group moved quickly within the stoopwalking initial passage leading to the squeeze. At the squeeze, Alex, Alex, and Sri decided to turn around and explore back to the lower entrance, past where we came in. The rest made their way through the squeeze, cursing the grippy a'a as it tore at their clothing (or fur). 

As the group made its way carefully through the passage covered in lava speleothems, we got separated slightly due to differing goals but luckily that was not an issue in a small linear cave.
While Randolph focused on recording his passage through the cave, the others focused on still photography and captured the beauty of the cavers, stalactites, stalagmites, lava splatter, and other formations (what's the term for lava helictites?)

We decided to turn back as the passage got smaller as nobody was really looking forward to more sharp lava crawls (except Cygnus) and after short discussion Randolph decided to take his own time on the way out to record. 

On the surface we met up with the others and returned to the cars, ready for more caving.
