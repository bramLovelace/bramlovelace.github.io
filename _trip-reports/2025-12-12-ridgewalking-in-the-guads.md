This summer while we were caving at the Marbles, Riannon mentioned that her airflow study in Carlsbad Caverns would be coming to an end in December and we were invited to help her tear down her stations and see the cave. 

I coordinated with Alex Fischer to get a ride from his place in Albuquerque down to the Guads before it and rode down with Kayla and Travis, two other Abq cavers, the weekend ahead of the fieldwork to check some small caves and leads. We met with Anastasiya and Evan from California and started hiking to Big Canyon the next morning. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/guads-1.jpg)

With the exception of Alex we struggled at first with the hostile desert plants but made acceptable time to the wash and an abandoned homestead at the entrance of the canyon. The wash provided a pleasant hike free of sharp and prickly plants as well as glimpses of shade until we arrived below the caves. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/guads-2.jpg)


The first cave we climbed up to was Big Canyon Boneyard and had been discovered in the late 20th century but not extensively pushed or surveyed. The cave had a large archway entrance and consisted of solidly cemented breakdown crawling that nobody had found a way past. A few weeks prior to our trip, Alex had surveyed the first few sections of it with some other locals and wanted us to push it and finish the survey. 

We split into two teams of two and started surveying where the previous team had left off. I sketched while Evan did point and Kayla learned instrument and we pushed through a tight squeeze and around a popcorn lined corner to a spot where an approximately 10cm diameter Rock hung down from the ceiling blocking forward movement. While it resonated nicely when hit with the hammer it would need feathers and wedges to be removed. We checked for air but did not feel any so we moved on to another lead and surveyed it around a small loop. At this point we joined the other team and checked a few small blind formation rooms before returning to the entrance to pack up and watch the Geminids.

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/guads-6.jpg)

Little darling cave is a small cave slightly up canyon from BCBC with extensive formations. We quickly set up to survey it with Alex sketching plan and X-section digitally and myself sketching profile. Once we got to the main room, I was sent down a squeeze below some formations and entered a small highly decorated room in which I was able to carefully slither to the end and determine did not continue. Since the squeeze was large enough, Kayla and Alex followed me into it and we added it to the survey. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/guads-3.jpg)


Meanwhile, Evan had found another squeeze to a separate room going down with a small pit. We surveyed it and agreed it was blind. Total cave length: 155’

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/guads-survey.jpg)

We returned to the wash and continued upstream towards a water source Alex had heard about and were happy to find several pools with water flowing between then and plenty of flat spaces to camp around it. I was happy to be cowboy camping and lost count at 30 meteors. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/guads-4.jpg)


The next morning the sun came late to the base of canyon and we had a slow start but eventually made it back to near the entrance of the canyon (including Alex finding another Barbary sheep skull). He had spotted three leads at the base of cliffs previously and marked them so we split up with him visiting the upper and the others checking two lower down. The first was easy to find and went about 15’ but did not continue. The second we searched for a while before turning back, likely because we were on too low a cliff band to find it. With none of the leads going we returned to the cars and made it just after dark. Hopefully one of the many cliff leads we spotted on the trip will go when rappelled into to allow use of this oasis camp. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/guads-5.jpg)

Alex and Kayla returned to Albuquerque while the rest of us checked into our house in Carlsbad ready for a week of fieldwork and fun.
