On Thursday I drove down to Portland and stayed with Drew. Friday I worked a little bit and took a meeting before leaving Portland at 10 and took a meeting on the road. Relatively uneventful drive. 

Made it to the trailhead to find the credit card machine wasn’t working so luckily had cash. Left the trailhead at 5:45pm and made it to hidden valley in about 1:45 with a stop at the horse camp to talk to the caretaker. Set up camp and packed for the summit day and  had enough time to sketch the view before dark and then slept from 10 to about 2. 

Woke up at 2 and had a slow morning, packed up stuff and collapsed the tent and left just before 3. 

Went up Cascade gulch and wasn’t feeling great but not terrible, made it to Shastina in 2:30 for sunrise and had some Tylenol which made my head better. Went back to the saddle and was not feeling good so had caffeine and forced myself to eat. (I noticed most of today I wasn’t able to eat much while at altitude). I looked at whitney glacier and decided it was a hell no (as expected) so went to the scramble which was the plan from the start. 

I was expecting some hard moves, as my experience with class 3 in the sierra was pretty sandbagged and this was described as 3/4 but it was relatively straightforward, even lacking the rotten volcanic rock I had expected. I tried to hug the bottom of the solid rock above he talus / scree pile and was pretty successful. A few places this would have been a large down climb so I took a gulley over and that worked well. 

I got to the end and, still wary of the snowfield heading to the glacier, skirted the edge (climbers right) after putting my crampons on until the Avy gulch route where I followed the summer trail up misery hill and then the boot pack across the snowfield to the summit block. At the top of misery hill the summit was in and out of the clouds. I summited at 10:20, 7.5 hours from camp. I stayed for about 30 mins eating (because I felt better), on my phone, and taking a few pictures. It was surprisingly calm and nobody else was there. I left and on the way down it was a whiteout and met a bunch of parties including one guy who had just climbed misery hill suggesting west face because Avy gulch was rockfalley and I was ahead of him. 
I had planned to take the shortcut from my GPS back to the west face gulch but I ended up taking the avy route down misery hill and a similar route to what I took up, skirting skiers left around the glacier to avoid it with the 20’(?) visibility. With gps help I made it to the top of west face which was steep and windy and still whiteout.

I walked carefully down using the suncups as my crampons weren’t fully grabbing in the warm snow. Once the snow cleared and I was at the first of two big boulders (check in pics?) I was able to take off my crampons and start glissading. The tracks weren’t the smoothest due to the suncups and there were a few faint chutes that I tried to stay in. Unfortunately my hardshell pants got a bunch of holes in the seat that will need fixing. 


I hiked back to the car quickly and made it just before 3pm, passing many day hikers, and took my time unpacking and drying stuff but just chose random t shirt and shorts to wear.

Overall it was a pretty good trip and the altitude didn’t affect me as much as I expected, maybe camping does help as well as actually sleeping. I felt worst at the saddle but the rest of the way only had a headache if I was pushing too fast. Overall had to slow down but that was good for pace. The other impact from altitude was lots of farts and intestinal distress when I decided I didn’t want to use the wag bag. Maybe I should have done that…


--- 
I stopped for food and gas in the town of Mt. Shasta before driving down to see the campsite with the SAG group. I was looking for cavers when i saw a group of people with Anastasiya hanging on rope from a tree which was a telltale sign. I was gonna climb first then come but i was already tired and didn’t want the extra driving and hiking so I’m guessing this will be my third time by Lassen without climbing it.

---

After a slow Sunday morning, I joined the some other cavers in a lava tube field and floated the idea of climbing Lassen. Luke Vroegop was interested so we headed towards the park. We got there and started hiking at around 2 after putting gear together. He was worried about the snow but I had both crampons and microspikes so we figured we would be able to make it. Only a few parts were snow covered and they were soft enough to not need spikes. Lots of tourists and a very chill trail to the top, we reached it in 1:45. 

While we were at the top, we saw two people exploring the other side of the crater and later heard a large rockfall which was right below them. We headed down and stopped a couple times to identify wildflowers (since Luke is a botanist for the forest service), it was interesting to see his process. We saw two people go by us and cut switchbacks and wander off trail which matched the ones we saw at the top likely. We got to the car by 5:15, making it a nice short hike. 

Worth the view but not worth travelling all they way down for, glad I tagged it while I was in the area.
