With summer  approaching I had been keeping an eye out for permits and spotted a couple weeks out a Friday available for the ever popular Boston Basin. Luc and I planned to leave Seattle early and make it to the trailhead at a reasonable time. 

Despite being snow free, cascade river road was closed at the Eldorado gate so we brought bikes to make the 2.5mi and 1kft to the Forbidden trailhead easier. With our heavy packs It took us about 45 minutes to reach the trailhead from the car, including stopping when we saw two black bears to let one walk past us (the other ran away into the woods). 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-1.JPEG)


We stashed our bikes and started up the trail. While it was clearly a climbers trail on Cascade River Road, it had a surprisingly small amount of straight up. We had been warned about the river crossings but luckily were able to get past them with no issues. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-2.JPEG)

We continued up past one tent (the only sign of humans we’d see the whole weekend off the road) and onto continuous snow. As it got dark we found a tent site in the upper basin at about 6300’. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-3.JPEG)

The mountains around us had been in and out of the clouds on our trip in Friday but when we woke up to leave on Saturday we were squarely in a whiteout. We delayed about an hour hoping that the sunrise would burn it off before deciding the visibility was good enough to continue. It improved slightly as we passed what looked at first like a glacier appeared just to be thick slabs of snow with massive glide cracks. It likely had been a glacier relatively recently. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-4.JPEG)

We got out our second tools and continued up a steep section to a flat area below the couloir where we roped up. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-5.JPEG)

Luc lead up the couloir and we did a mix of running belay and pitching it out with the pickets and rappel stations before we reached the end of the snow at the notch in the west ridge where we left our snow gear. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-6.JPEG)

We had brought a double rack so I took all of the pro and Luc belayed me out about 20m before starting climbing. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-7.JPEG)

This was our first time simul climbing and ended up working great for a route like this which is mostly scramblable with a few exposed 5th class moves. Some trip reports mention a part that was necessary to pitch out but we never found that point so I climbed to where I ran out of gear just below the false summit. From there  I belayed Luc in and he finished the (down) climb to the summit. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-8.JPEG)

The whiteout continued on the south side all but the north side cooperated better, giving us glimpses down to the Boston glacier and Moraine lake. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-9.JPEG)

After a relatively short amount of time on the summit we started the climb back down the ridge with Luc in the lead. We made it to the notch after a few rappels and a few more sections of downclimbing. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-10.JPEG)

Instead of following the gulley rappel route, we decided to retrace our steps up the couloir as we had seen many rappel stations on our way up and weren’t sure the gulley was out of the snow. This turned out to be a mistake as we ended up having to add a lot of faff finding stations either just out of reach or requiring scrambling to get to. The snow was softening up and we both had our legs fall into holes that had been covered by snow bridges in the morning. If we’d gone a month earlier I would have much rather skied down because the couloir was plenty wide to comfortably make turns and at an angle to be fun skiing. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-11.JPEG)

There were so many rappel stations in disrepair or unreachable that we lost count which is a huge detriment to the beauty and safety on a 50 classic climb. Someone should go and clean it up and replace them with bolts to lower the wilderness impact if the park service hadn’t chopped them when someone did that over a decade ago. [Article on this](https://washingtonclimbers.org/index.php/2014/09/01/12534/)

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-11a.JPEG)


We eventually made it to the more open snow and did two additional rappels that were much more straightforward to bypass the steep section below the couloir. These were likely the bottom of the correct descent beta. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-12.JPEG)


From there it was an easy walk down to the tent and a quick hike out of the clouds and back to the road. The bikes were amazing here, taking only 10 minutes to get back to the car and passing two groups hiking down. We had beat the after work crowds Friday but Saturday night the parking lot was bustling with people planning alpine starts with many skis and bikes around. 

We were able to get back to Seattle around midnight. 

It was a great climb. I see why it’s in the 50 Classic Climbs of North America even being in whiteout for the most scenic part. That was  made up for by having such a popular  mountain to ourselves all day. 

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bramlovelace/bramlovelace.github.io/master/images/forbidden-13.jpg)

Forbidden is my 8th Bulger and 20th Smoot. Fun to keep track but not sure I’m really going all out to finish the lists. Excluding repeating Baker and MSH, it’s the first summit out of three Bulger attempts this year. Winter is a hard time to successfully reach high summits but the skiing down is fun regardless. (The blue line above is an Eldorado attempt that became a mellow ski day due to avalanche conditions).
